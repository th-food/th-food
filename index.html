Foods to Avoid with Thyroid Medication

It is important to follow proper timing when consuming food relative to thyroid medication, as certain foods may interfere with the absorption of the medication.

Food Timing Reason
Calcium-Rich Foods (e.g., Dairy, Leafy Greens)Avoid within 1 hour of medicationCalcium can interfere with thyroid hormone absorption.
Iron-Rich Foods (e.g., Red Lentils, Spinach)Avoid within 1 hour of medicationIron can decrease medication effectiveness.
Soy-Based Foods (e.g., Tofu, Soy Milk)Avoid within 4 hours of medicationSoy can block thyroid hormone absorption.
High-Fiber Foods (e.g., Bran, Oats)Avoid within 1 hour of medicationHigh fiber can bind to thyroid hormones and decrease absorption.
Foods High in Goitrogens (e.g., Raw Broccoli, Cauliflower)Avoid if eating raw, cooked is fine after waiting 30-60 minutesGoitrogens may interfere with thyroid function, especially when eaten raw.
Milk & Dairy ProductsFine to consume, but wait at least 30-60 minutes after medicationMilk does not interfere as long as it is consumed after a waiting period.
